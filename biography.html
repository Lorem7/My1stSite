Oleg Vladimirovich
Pozidaev

Oleg was born in the city of Vladivostok, in the far east of Russia in 1973. When he was yet five years of age his mother noticed that her son showed abilities for drawing, and it was at that time that Oleg stated that he would be an artist. At the age of ten he enrolled in a local art school for kids, where his natural talent blossomed under the guidance of the skilled artist Galina Stepanovna Tsapko.

At the age of 14 he entered an Art Collage, painting and art teachers department in Vladivostok where he received professional skills and a degree. The artist was especially impressed by the love for landscape and still life paintings. Trying to utilize any spare moment with his easel he would leave the city to go to the countryside art retreats to do nature study drawings.

In 1993 Oleg was invited to work as an illustrator at the International printing company, Bhaktivedanta Book Trust. Besides his work on the book illustrations he constantly participated at the regional and urban exhibitions in different cities of Russia – Moscow, Vladivostok, Sochi, and Krasnodar. His friends and colleagues notes that Oleg's art is filled with a poetry of sunshine and warmth that gives a spectator high positive emotions and joy. The artist works exclusively in a classical style of realism in which details are portrayed very delicately and where the atmosphere and entirety of vision creates an impression of unified environment.

From 1996 -1997 Oleg traveled throughout India with his sketchbook and paints doing sketches from nature and collecting material for his paintings about ancient India. In 2001 the artist was invited to create mural art in a Hindu temple on Mauritius Island. With the help of his wife It took seven years to complete the project.

Inspired by the nature of a tropical country he did life drawings of Mauritius natural scenes and exhibited his paintings at local galleries. Today you can find his paintings in private collections in France, Italy, Switzerland, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Mauritius Island, and Russia.

In 2009 Oleg Pozidaev was invited to work on the interior decoration in Egyptian art style for the Five Star Aton Hotel in the city of Krasnodar. It took him four and a half years to decorate the Hotel with 100 square meters of monumental mural art. In 2013 the artist then started working on large scale colorful copy paintings for the same hotel, depicting the life of ancient Egypt. he regularly exhibits his paintings at the art events of Artists' Union (ART-Union) in Krasnodar City.

At the present moment the artist lives in the southern part of Russia, in the foothills of the Caucasus near the city of Goryachii Klutch where he tries to capture the beauty of local nature in his fine art paintings.
